Definition: Closed questions are questions that can be answered with a simple 'yes' or 'no'. They are often used to check understanding or to get a quick confirmation. | Case #1: Using 'be' verb | You are a student. -> Are you a student? / She is from Canada. -> Is she from Canada?
Case #2: Using 'do' verb | You like coffee. -> Do you like coffee? / He plays tennis. -> Does he play tennis?
Case #3: Using 'have' verb | You have a car. -> Do you have a car? / She has a dog. -> Does she have a dog?
Case #4: Using modal verbs | You can swim. -> Can you swim? / He should go. -> Should he go?
Case #5: Using past tense | You went to the party. -> Did you go to the party? / She finished her work. -> Did she finish her work?^^
