Definition: Question tags are short questions at the end of statements. They are used to confirm information or to encourage a reply. The basic structure is: positive statement, negative tag; negative statement, positive tag. | Case #1: Positive statement, negative tag | You are a student, -> aren't you? / He has finished his homework, -> hasn't he?
Case #2: Negative statement, positive tag | You aren't a student, -> are you? / He hasn't finished his homework, -> has he?
Case #3: With 'be' verb | She is from Canada, -> isn't she? / They weren't at home, -> were they?
Case #4: With 'have' verb | You have a car, -> don't you? / She has a dog, -> doesn't she?
Case #5: With modal verbs | He can swim, -> can't he? / You should go, -> shouldn't you?
Case #6: With imperatives | Close the door, -> will you? / Don't be late, -> will you?
Case #7: With 'let's' | Let's go to the beach, -> shall we? / Let's have a party, -> shall we?
Case #8: With 'I am' | I am your friend, -> aren't I? / I am late, -> aren't I?^^
