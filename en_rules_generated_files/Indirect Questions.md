Definition: Indirect questions are polite forms of direct questions. They are often used in more formal situations or when asking a question to a stranger. The word order in an indirect question is the same as in a statement, not a question. | Case #1: Starting with 'Could you tell me...' | Direct: Where is the station? -> Indirect: Could you tell me where the station is? / Direct: What time does the bank open? -> Indirect: Could you tell me what time the bank opens?
Case #2: Starting with 'Do you know...' | Direct: Is he coming to the party? -> Indirect: Do you know if he is coming to the party? / Direct: Did she finish her work? -> Indirect: Do you know if she finished her work?
Case #3: Starting with 'I was wondering...' | Direct: Will you be home later? -> Indirect: I was wondering if you will be home later. / Direct: Can you help me with this? -> Indirect: I was wondering if you could help me with this.
Case #4: Starting with 'I'd like to know...' | Direct: How much does this cost? -> Indirect: I'd like to know how much this costs. / Direct: When is the next train? -> Indirect: I'd like to know when the next train is.
Case #5: Embedded in a statement | The question is how we can solve the problem. -> The question is how we can solve the problem. / I'm not sure where I left my keys. -> I'm not sure where I left my keys.^^
