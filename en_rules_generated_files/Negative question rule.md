Definition: Negative questions are formed with a negative contraction (e.g., isn't, don't, can't) and are used to confirm information you believe to be true, express surprise, or make suggestions. | Case #1: With 'be' verb (Confirmation) | I thought this was your book. -> Isn't this your book? / I expected to see you at the party last night. -> Weren't you at the party last night?
Case #2: With 'do' verb (Surprise/Disbelief) | You should see the sign right in front of you. -> Don't you see the sign? / He was supposed to tell you he was leaving. -> Didn't he tell you he was leaving?
Case #3: With 'have' verb (Checking Information) | You were supposed to finish the report by now. -> Haven't you finished the report yet? / I was expecting her to call you back. -> Hasn't she called you back?
Case #4: With Modal Verbs (Suggestions/Politeness) | I think we should talk about this later. -> Can't we talk about this later? / You really need to be studying for your exam. -> Shouldn't you be studying for your exam?
Case #5: As Suggestions (Why don't...) | I think going out for dinner is a good idea. -> Why don't we go out for dinner? / You could have asked for help. -> Why didn't you ask for help?
Case #6: Expressing Opinion/Rhetorical Questions | It would be nice if we had a day off. -> Wouldn't it be nice if we had a day off? / That is the most ridiculous thing I've ever heard. -> Isn't that the most ridiculous thing you've ever heard?^^
